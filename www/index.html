Chiller plant retrofits are often the centerpiece of Carrier’s energy retrofit business. Through collaboration and innovation, our unique approach and creative solutions won’t box you in, but open up new possibilities. Together, our expertise and products enable you to integrate systems, optimize performance and adapt to new applications and operating scenarios.

Ask the experts at Carrier to design and implement Chiller Plant Optimization Strategies that reliably deliver real and significant energy savings, running under the operating conditions encountered in everyday life.

System Approach: Chiller Plant Energy Savings Strategies

  • Measuring and analyzing the chiller plant load profile
  • Modeling a plant retrofit will maximize the economic benefit and performance
  • Pumps, cooling towers, boilers, automation, power distribution, and air distribution apparatus are critical and integral to our analysis of chiller plant performance

System Performance
Optimize total plant energy and not Individual system components
System Reliability
Understand the interaction energy savings strategies have on other system components in plant operation
Significant Energy Savings
Accurate, appropriate metrics

Project Approach:

  • Single Point of Contact: Dedicated Team for Each Project
  • Comprehensive Survey Report
  • Work with your Engineer on pre-design
  • Engineered Design/Skid Systems
  • Phased Equipment Replacement Proposal
  • SOW Assistance
  • Turn-Key Installation of all disciplines to complete project
  • Project Management
  • ROI Energy Analysis
  • Start up Commissioning and Warranty
  • Conference Call on Options at Every Milestone
  • Turn-Key accountability

Carrier offers support for every stage of a chiller plant’s lifecycle to reduce chiller plant operation, increase reliability, and extend the life of your capital investment.

Application of Technology to Meet Objectives:

  • Look for Heat recovery savings
  • Convert constant flow system into variable flow to increase part load efficiency (air, water, and refrigerant)
  • Systems older than 10 to 15 years offer an investment opportunity. Install new high-efficiency equipment or retrofits are available to reduce operating costs and match equipment size to cooling load
  • Use airside and waterside free cooling when possible
  • Transfer cooling load from chillers to cooling tower when possible
  • Automation to take advantage of improved part load performance

Carrier’s chiller plant upgrades often include modifications to existing equipment. Our understanding of the performance of chilled water equipment allows us to model and implement modifications that improve the performance and extend the life of existing chillers. Existing chiller retrofits include, but are not limited to the following:

  • Compressor modification (impeller, motor, shroud, transmission, turbine, etc.)
  • Variable speed drive
  • Retube
  • Refrigerant conversion
  • Driveline retrofit
  • Control sequencing
  • Flow control

Pump systems represent a significant opportunity for improving the performance of most existing buildings. By closely matching the pump performance of building hydronic systems with the heat transfer requirements of HVAC equipment, Carrier is able to deliver significant improvement to building performance.

We have, in many instances, made use of low temperature/low flow and free cooling systems to minimize pumping costs and to optimize heat transfer efficiency of HVAC components.

Carrier values the strategic partnership we have with Hilton Worldwide. It is our goal to support your HVAC upgrades that arise in your properties with the latest technology backed by ROI savings and expertise to provide you the best solution on time, on budget with phased project approach to deliver an optimized chiller plant.

Depending on which product is being registered, a user may be required to give their contact information such as name, address, email address, company name, company address, phone number, the model and serial number of the product being registered, and computer system information, such as operating system type, operating system version, and operating system language. BIS may request and collect optional demographic information
